1. Getting Started

Before using the application daily, the system must be initialized by creating the primary administrator account and setting up baseline data.

Registration and Login

Navigate to the application URL in your web browser. If no admin account exists, you will be prompted to create one.

  • Username: Desired admin username (e.g., admin)
  • Password: Ensure you use a strong password (e.g., adminadmin for local environments)

Importing Example Data

To populate the system with placeholder rooms, rates, and test bookings, navigate to the Settings page and trigger the data import.

  1. From the sidebar navigation, click on Settings.
  2. Locate the Import example data button.
  3. Click the button and confirm your action. The dashboard will now reflect the newly imported data.

2. Dashboard

The Dashboard is the central hub of your MiniHotel system, offering an at-a-glance overview of your property's performance.

  • Occupancy Metrics: View current and projected occupancy rates.
  • Revenue: Track daily or monthly revenue figures.
  • Recent Activity: See the latest bookings and status changes.

3. Bookings

Manage all guest reservations in the Bookings module. Here, you can create new bookings, modify existing ones, and manage check-ins/check-outs.

4. Calendar

The Calendar provides a visual timeline of all reservations across your rooms. It's the most intuitive way to spot availability gaps and overlapping bookings.

  • Drag and drop reservations (if supported) to adjust dates.
  • Click on a timeline block to view booking details immediately.

5. Rooms Management

Configure your physical property inventory. In this section, you define the rooms available for booking.

Action Description
Add Room Create a new room number/name, assign a type, and set details.
Edit Status Mark rooms as active, under maintenance, or inactive.

6. Rates & Pricing

Define how much a room costs based on dates, seasons, or room types.

The rates module allows you to set standard pricing and promotional or seasonal rate overrides to maximize revenue.

7. Services & Add-ons

Add extra items or services that guests can purchase during their stay. Examples include:

  • Breakfast or meal plans
  • Airport transfers
  • Spa treatments or equipment rentals

8. Reports

Generate detailed insights into your hotel's operations. Access financial summaries, occupancy trends, and tax reports to aid in management decisions.

9. Housekeeping

Manage the cleanliness and readiness of your rooms. Track which rooms need cleaning following a checkout or during a guest's stay.

10. Audit Logs

Maintain security and accountability. The audit log tracks system events, such as user logins, data modifications, and critical system alerts.
